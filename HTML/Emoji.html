This module consists of a Apple monitor with a stand. But the stand is also by Apple, so it costs $999. In frustration, you decide you want to destroy it. However, if you do not destory it correctly it will rematerialize (and give you a strike).

The monitor consists of two emoji, both of which have a name. Click the two emoji in the image below to get their names for the next step.

Using the names of the two emoji, create new grid where it's rows have the letters of the left emoji from top to bottom, and it's columns have the letters of the right emoji from left to right. Using the column as the first character and the row as the second, determine whether these two characters form a valid two-letter code representing an emoji flag. These flags are found below. Consider valid codes as filled in the grid, and invalid as filled.

A region will be defined as any orthogonally adjacent cells in your generated grid of similar shade (filled or unfilled). Count the number of regions total in your grid. For example, if both your grid's columns and rows had the entire alphabet as their names, the resulting grid would have 69 regions.

The number of regions in your grid determines how many times to punch each emoji on the screen. The tens digit determines how many times you punch the left emoji, and the ones digit determines how many times you punch the right emoji.

Failure to do this correctly will yield a strike, rematerialize the monitor, and give you new emoji.
